00Where Bitloom stands today

The honest assessment that everything below is built on.

Assets & strengths

  • Founder: Miroslav — 7+ yrs senior full-stack (TS/React/Next.js/Node/React Native, AWS serverless, PostgreSQL), now specialised in AI engineering (LangGraph.js, Claude, MCP, CrewAI, RAG).
  • Domain credibility: fintech, banking, healthcare/pharma, enterprise — high-stakes, regulated sectors. Rare and valuable.
  • Proof: 3 strong LinkedIn-verified testimonials, 10+ projects, public GitHub portfolio (AI research agent, CrewAI image analyzer, RN apps).
  • Channels in place: bilingual EN/SK site, a blog (blog.bitloom.sk), LinkedIn company page, GA4 analytics, Košice/EU base.
  • Writes well; sits squarely in the hottest niche of 2026 (AI agents / MCP).

Constraints & reality

  • Solo + time-constrained. Every hour spent here competes with billable work.
  • No existing audience yet at scale — the single biggest gate on "passive" income (see below).
  • "Passive" is mostly a myth near-term: every documented success had an audience or a client base first.
  • The €200–300/mo target is modest and very achievable — it's the bottom-to-middle of every distribution we researched, not the unicorn tail.

The one finding that shapes the whole strategy

Across all three research tracks, the same truth surfaced independently: recurring income follows an existing relationship or audience — it does not appear before one. So the fastest path to €200–300/mo is the one where Bitloom already has the relationship (clients), and the most durable path is the one that builds a compounding asset (an audience). The two are complementary, not either/or.

02Option 1 — Retainers & new clients Recommended start

The lowest-effort, highest-probability route. This is "semi-passive" rather than passive — but a single retainer already clears the goal.

The core move: turn project work into monthly recurring revenue

Industry maintenance pricing runs 15–25% of the original build cost per year; productized "care plans" cluster at $100–250/month (mid-tier 2025 average ≈ $246/mo). That means one or two clients on a care plan hits the entire €200–300 target — and only ~13% of consultants even offer retainers, so the upside is largely untapped.

Realistic /mo €100–250 per client
To hit goal 1–2 retainers
Time to revenue Weeks (existing clients)
Ongoing effort A few hrs/mo per client

How to structure it

  • 2–3 tiers: e.g. Basic ~€100/mo (monitoring + minor fixes), Standard ~€250/mo (priority + small features), Premium ~€500/mo. Discounted hour-blocks below your project rate.
  • Pitch at the moment of peak trust — immediately after a successful project handoff. Framework: reference results → name the recurring challenges → present the plan → propose a trial period.
  • Protect yourself: defined monthly hours, response-time SLA, scope exclusions, rollover rules, termination clause — to stop scope creep.

Where to find the first clients

Best-fit marketplaces for a senior AI + EU dev:

  • Lemon.io — Eastern-Europe focused, ~$60–120/hr, strong EU fit, vets clients (guaranteed payment). Best EU entry
  • Toptal / A.Team — high rates ($60–200+/hr; AI specialists $200+/hr), long engagements = recurring-friendly. Slow, selective vetting. Note: Toptal embeds a hidden ~10–20% markup, so you net less than the client pays.
  • Braintrust / Contra — 0% talent fee, keep 100%; more self-driven acquisition.
  • Upwork — good to land a first client; heavy price competition; fees now 0–15%.

Local & outbound channels (Košice / CET / EU SMBs)

  • TITANS.sk — Slovakia's IT freelancer-outsourcing marketplace (subcontracting).
  • Košice IT Valley cluster + local meetups (Innovation & Start-up Community, Cloud) — warm, in-city leads.
  • LinkedIn outbound (highest-ROI cold channel): warm-first, 15–20 connection requests/day, lead with a free AI/website audit as a lead magnet. DMs reply ~10% vs ~5% for cold email.

Market tailwind: Only ~17% of small EU enterprises use AI (vs 55% of large firms) — a low-saturation regional market for hands-on AI-automation help.

The differentiated upsell: a "productized AI Automation" package

This is Bitloom's clearest edge into the underserved EU-SMB gap. Fixed scope, flat pricing: typical builds run $2k–10k + monthly retainers of $500–1,500 (basic) and up. Even a single small automation retainer comfortably exceeds the starting goal and is far more defensible than generic dev work.

03Option 2 — Build a focused product

Higher ceiling and true semi-passive potential, but front-loaded and with real failure odds. Build narrow, in your domain — not a generic boilerplate or a thin AI wrapper.

Reality check before building anything

Median micro-SaaS earns ~$500/mo; ~70% earn under $500/mo and ~54% of indie products make $0. Your €200–300 band (≈10–30 customers) is realistic — but it is not passive; it needs active distribution. Good news: AI coding tools now make an MVP shippable in ~3 weeks for near-zero cost, and AI-first products reach $1k/mo materially faster than traditional software. Also: price higher and sell to businesses — sub-$25/mo products churn ~6%/mo; aim for ~10–15 customers at €20–40/mo, not 100 at €3.

⭐ Bitloom already has a head start: ReadNest

ReadNest (in repos/readnest, not yet on the Bitloom site) is a polished, feature-complete, well-tested Expo / React Native app for tracking books and capturing insights from them — local-first, privacy-first, no account needed. It's ~2–3 weeks of store-listing work away from shipping. The expensive part — a tested, CI-backed, near-MVP app — is already built and paid for. That makes it the cheapest possible experiment for the entire product path.

Status Near-MVP, buildable today (no backend needed)
Current monetization Buy Me a Coffee donation only
As-is potential ~$10–50/mo (donation, not the goal)
To reach goal Needs a real paid tier

The honest catch: a donation model won't reliably hit €200–300/mo — a hard paywall earns ~8× donation/freemium, but even then it's top-15–20%-of-apps territory, and the real bottleneck is distribution/ASO in a crowded book-tracking category, not the code.

The unlock that fits Bitloom: the natural premium tier is AI features (insight summarization, auto-tagging, reflection prompts) — which plays directly to Miroslav's AI-engineering edge and can be a hard paywall via RevenueCat (~2–4 weeks). Avoid paid cloud-sync first: it requires building the empty backend (6–10 wks) and breaks the privacy-first story.

Recommended play: (1) ship it now with the donation model — nearly free, yields real install/retention data, and becomes a live portfolio piece + lead-gen asset (a finished AI-ready mobile app to show clients) regardless of its own revenue; (2) add an AI-powered Pro tier only after retention data justifies it; (3) treat its main value as learning app-store monetization cheaply + credibility, with direct revenue as upside — not as the dependable route to the goal (that's still retainers, Option 1).

How you'd actually get the first 10–30 customers

Works: value-first niche communities (Indie Hackers, targeted subreddits, AI Discords), build-in-public, tightly-targeted outreach, and citation-earning technical/comparison content. Overrated: Product Hunt (featured rate collapsed to ~10%; a launch yields maybe 10–20 paying customers — good for one-time awareness, not your customer base). Avoid: thin programmatic SEO, generic content vs Google AI Overviews.

Verify before betting

Reports of an official Anthropic paid Skills/plugin marketplace (15% cut) circulated but could only be traced to low-quality speculative blogs — treat as unconfirmed; verify at claude.com/plugins. Real third-party MCP monetization does exist (e.g. MCPize ~85% rev-share, and emerging per-call rails like x402 / Stripe MPP), but it's fragmented and early — fine as a credibility/lead-gen line, risky as a sole revenue base.

Why it matters even though it's slow: the audience is the one asset that compounds and makes the product (Option 2) and the client pipeline (Option 1) dramatically easier. Starting it now is the highest-leverage decision in this brief.

05Recommendation

Don't pick one option — run them in the right order. Use the fastest path to hit the number now (retainers), and in parallel start the slow compounding asset (audience) that will make a product viable later.

✅ Do first (hit €200–300 in weeks)

  • Package 2–3 maintenance / care-plan tiers and pitch every past & future client at handoff. 1–2 yeses = goal met.
  • Build the "AI Automation Setup" productized offer — Bitloom's sharpest differentiator into the EU-SMB gap.
  • Open one acquisition channel: Lemon.io + warm LinkedIn outreach with a free-audit lead magnet.

🌱 Start in parallel (compounds for later)

  • Launch a free beehiiv newsletter in the AI-engineering / MCP niche. This is the long-term engine.
  • Write one technical guide as the lead magnet (also resells later as an ebook).
  • Once the audience exists, build Idea A (EU-compliant MCP/AI toolkit) — it sells to that audience and inherits the trust.

This sequencing means Bitloom doesn't gamble months of build time on a product before having any way to distribute it — and doesn't stay stuck in pure hourly work with no compounding asset. The retainer covers the goal immediately; the audience makes everything after it easier.

06Suggested 90-day roadmap

Weeks 1–2 — Bank the quick win

Define care-plan tiers (~€100 / €250 / €500). Email every past and current client a retainer/care-plan offer at the next natural touchpoint. Target: first €200–300/mo signed.

Weeks 1–6 — Open the pipeline

Apply to Lemon.io (+ optionally Toptal/A.Team). Run warm LinkedIn outreach (15–20/day) leading with a free AI/website audit to Slovak/CET SMBs. Stand up the "AI Automation Setup" package page.

Weeks 2–8 — Plant the audience

Launch the beehiiv newsletter; publish the first technical guide as a lead magnet. Repurpose existing GitHub projects (research agent, CrewAI analyzer) into build-in-public posts. Goal: consistent weekly publishing rhythm, not subscriber count yet.

Weeks 2–5 — Ship ReadNest (cheap product experiment)

Finish store-listing assets and publish ReadNest (Android + iOS) with the donation model — it's nearly free and already built. Add it to the Bitloom site as a live portfolio/lead-gen piece. The goal here is install + retention data and learning the app-store game, not immediate revenue.

Months 2–3 — Validate a product wedge

If ReadNest retention looks promising, add an AI-powered Pro tier (RevenueCat hard paywall) — leveraging Miroslav's AI edge. In parallel, spec a thin MVP of Idea A (EU-compliant MCP/RAG toolkit); talk to 5–10 target EU dev teams before building, ship only after a demand signal. Add affiliate links (Cloudways/Kit/Make) to the newsletter.

Month 3+ — Stack & compound

Add a paid starter-kit/template for semi-passive income. Grow the newsletter toward the 2,500-sub sponsorship threshold. Convert more clients to retainers. Each layer makes the next cheaper to acquire.
