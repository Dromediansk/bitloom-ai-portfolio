## Summary

- **No conversion tracking exists.** GA4 has zero key events configured — `keyEvents = 0` on every page and channel, the contact form has no `form_submit`/inquiry event, and the `click` event fired only 4 times in 30 days. The site's stated goal is lead generation, yet **leads are completely invisible in analytics**. This is the #1 issue and a prerequisite for everything else.
- **Traffic is very low and statistically noisy:** 22 users / 30 sessions in the current 30 days. Single visitors swing every percentage, so treat all deltas below as directional, not precise.
- **The headline decline is partly an artifact.** Sessions fell 30 → 69 (-57%) and page views 30 → 137 (-78%), but the prior window was inflated by `vercel.com` referral (11 sessions / **1 user** — self or bot) and a `hashnode.com` referral burst. Stripping those out, the *real* drop is much smaller.
- **Organic search is the only durable, meaningful channel** (~70% of current traffic) and it is essentially stable (21 vs 25 sessions). Nearly all of it lands on **two technical blog posts** — Expo/Supabase Google Sign-In and EU web accessibility.
- **The blog earns the audience; the marketing site does not.** Portfolio pages (home, services, projects) get almost no organic traffic, and the `/en` home shows only ~2.6s dwell. There is no measured bridge from engaged blog readers to the contact form.

### Prioritized recommendations

1. **Configure GA4 key events for the contact form (`form_submit` + a contact-intent click) and mark them as conversions.** — `keyEvents = 0` everywhere and `click` fired only 4×, so leads are invisible despite lead-gen being the site's goal → makes leads measurable for the first time, so every action below can be judged by lead lift instead of vanity sessions, and unlocks channel-level lead attribution.
2. **Register custom dimensions and attach a "lead source / first landing page" parameter to the contact submit.** — No custom dimensions are registered, so no event-parameter breakdowns are possible → once leads are tracked you'll see *which* blog post actually produces inquiries, letting you double down on content that converts rather than content that merely gets views.
3. **Add contextual "blog → service" CTAs inside the two high-performing posts, each linking to the matching service + contact form.** — 100% of meaningful organic traffic lands on two technical posts while portfolio pages get near-zero organic traffic, with no measured bridge to contact → converts the only audience you have into inquiries. Match intent: Expo/Supabase post → "Need a React Native / Expo app built?" → mobile services; accessibility post → "Need your site to meet the European Accessibility Act? Let's audit it" → web services.
4. **Productize an "EU Accessibility / EAA compliance audit" as a named service with its own SK + EN landing page.** — The accessibility post shows 100% engagement and 334s dwell (far above everything else) proving demand, yet there's no service for that intent to land on → captures high-intent demand into a packaged, deadline-driven, billable offer instead of dead-ending engaged readers on a blog post.
5. **Rewrite the portfolio home above-the-fold around a specific, niche value proposition.** — `/en` home dwell is only ~2.6s vs 158s avg session and 334s on the accessibility post → a specific headline (healthcare/fintech precision + accessibility/mobile expertise) should lift engagement and cut instant exits; avoid generic "we build great software" copy.
6. **Add bot/internal-traffic filters and exclude your own Vercel preview/dev domains; treat the −57% drop as mostly a measurement artifact.** — Prior window's `vercel.com/referral` was 11 sessions / 1 user and organic search is actually stable → prevents over-reacting to a fake decline and makes future numbers trustworthy.
7. **Publish more SEO blog content on the two proven themes (Expo/React Native/Supabase; web accessibility), with proper Slovak versions.** — Organic search is the only growing channel and both winners are narrow technical/compliance topics that rank → compounds your one working channel; adjacent posts (Expo push notifications, Supabase RLS, an EAA checklist for Slovak businesses) extend a keyword footprint that already attracts qualified readers. Write Slovak natively (not Czech-influenced) to capture the local market EN-only posts miss.
8. **Add a low-friction secondary CTA (checklist download / lightweight email capture) on blog and service pages, tracked as its own key event.** — Traffic is tiny and the only conversion path today is the full project-inquiry form → captures warm readers not yet ready for a full inquiry, building a nurturable pipeline you currently lose entirely.

## Data quality & caveats

- **Very low traffic:** 22 users / 30 sessions in the current window — too few for statistical reliability. Every percentage delta is driven by a handful of visits; read trends, not precision.
- **No conversion/key events configured:** `keyEvents = 0` across all pages and channels. The contact form has no `form_submit`/inquiry event, and only 4 `click` events fired in 30 days — leads and outbound clicks are effectively untracked. Fixing this (Marketing #1) is the prerequisite for meaningful future analysis.
- **No custom dimensions/metrics registered:** event-parameter breakdowns (e.g. by blog post or CTA label) are not queryable until dimensions are registered in GA4 Admin.
- **Prior-window inflation:** `vercel.com/referral` contributed 11 sessions from a single user (self/preview/bot), and a `hashnode.com` referral burst added more — both inflate the prior baseline and exaggerate the apparent decline. Add internal/bot traffic filters.
- **Attribution gaps:** 6 current sessions have `(not set)` landing pages.
- **Title anomaly:** a `/sk` view reported the title "Online hodiny španielčiny pre začiatočníkov aj pokročilých" — verify the SK route's metadata.
- **Read-only:** this analysis only reads GA data; no GA configuration was changed.
